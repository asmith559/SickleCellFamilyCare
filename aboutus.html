About Us

Our mission is to support families affected by Sickle Cell Disease

Our Story

The Sickle Cell Disease & Awareness Hub was born from a deep commitment to supporting individuals and families affected by Sickle Cell Disease (SCD). Recognizing the challenges, both medical and emotional, that come with this condition, we set out to create a space where education, advocacy, and community come together. Our journey began with a simple yet powerful goal: to bridge the gap between awareness and action, ensuring that those living with SCD have access to the resources, support, and knowledge they need.

We understand that SCD is not just a medical condition; it affects every aspect of a person's life. From the struggles of managing chronic pain to the need for better health care access, we are here to amplify the voices of warriors, caregivers, and advocates. Our platform is dedicated to sharing stories, promoting cutting-edge research, and fostering a supportive community that drives change.

My Story

My name is Ashley Smith, and I am a mother of a 14-year-old warrior, Marcus Brooks Jr, who lives with both autism and Sickle Cell disease (SS). Our journey with Sickle Cell has been filled with countless challenges, hospitalizations, and medical procedures, each one shaping our resilience and deepening my commitment to advocacy.

Over the years, Marcus has faced a series of serious health complications due to Sickle Cell Disease. He has undergone multiple surgeries, including the removal of his gallbladder, spleen, tonsils, and adenoids. He's had a port placed, endured more than twenty hospitalizations for acute chest syndrome, received numerous blood transfusions, both simple and exchange transfusions, and continues to battle the unpredictable nature of this disease. Now, we are preparing for a new chapter as Marcus embarks on a journey with gene therapy, a cutting-edge treatment that we hope will change the course of his life.

Through this platform, I aim to bring awareness to the realities of living with Sickle Cell Disease not just from a medical standpoint, but from a deeply personal one. I will share our journey, blogging about the highs and lows, the triumphs and the setbacks, to give an honest and unfiltered look at what it means to live inside this disease, both as a parent and through Marcus's experience as a patient.

Our journey is unique because, in addition to managing Sickle Cell, Marcus also has autism. This has meant advocating even more fiercely to ensure he not only receives proper medical care but also understands what he's going through in a way that makes sense to him. Balancing both conditions has required an immense amount of strength, patience, and determination.

But I am not just advocating for my son; I am advocating for every individual and family affected by Sickle Cell Disease. I want to take this community with us on our journey as we explore better treatment options, navigate the complexities of this condition, and work toward what we all hope forâ€”a cure. My hope is that by sharing our story, we can foster a greater understanding of Sickle Cell Disease, provide support to those who need it, and inspire meaningful change in the fight for better care, research, and resources.

Together, we can raise awareness, push for advancements, and build a future where no one faces Sickle Cell alone.

Community Impact

Our Initiatives Include:

  • Awareness Campaigns

    Challenging misconceptions and promoting understanding of Sickle Cell Disease.

  • Support Networks

    Connecting individuals and families to share comfort, encouragement, and lived experiences.

  • Medical Collaboration

    Working with medical experts to deliver accurate, science-backed information and updates on new treatments.

We believe that by working together, we can break barriers, foster inclusivity, and create a future where no one with Sickle Cell Disease feels alone or unheard. Our commitment is unwavering, and our passion is driven by the resilience of the SCD community.

Our Mission and Goals

Our mission is to empower, educate, and advocate for individuals and families affected by Sickle Cell Disease. We are committed to:

Raising Awareness

Providing reliable, accessible, and up-to-date information about Sickle Cell Disease, treatment options, and advancements in research.

Advocacy & Policy Change

Collaborating with healthcare professionals, policymakers, and organizations to improve healthcare policies and increase funding for Sickle Cell research.

Community Support

Offering a platform where patients, caregivers, and advocates can share experiences, find support, and build meaningful connections.

Health Education

Equipping individuals with the knowledge and tools they need to manage Sickle Cell Disease effectively, including lifestyle tips, treatment options, and mental health support.

Blood Donations & Genetic Testing

Highlighting the crucial role of blood donations and early genetic screening in improving the lives of those with SCD.

Sickle Cell Family Care

Supporting families affected by Sickle Cell Disease with resources, education, and community.
